A bit about me

I’m a recent Computer Science graduate from the University of Sheffield, determined to become a software engineer.

I’ve always been drawn to and fascinated by technology since I was small. I think I was born at the perfect time to see its progression, whether that be the evolution of the internet, mobile phones or gaming. My aspiration to become a software engineer was progressive. It started from my silly phone and gaming interest to choosing ICT in GCSEs, to choosing A-Level Computing and Mathematics and then completing a Computer Science degree.

In my personal statement for university, I said: “The first program I ever created contained seven lines: a console application that simply reversed a string. Although it was a very basic program, the sense of achievement and satisfaction that I felt could not have been greater.” I was wrong - it could be greater and that’s when you’re developing for someone else and for real. At university and working at Feastly Ltd, I learnt that a happy client means a happy Ting. It’s much more enjoyable and satisfying when you know what you’re creating is going to be seen and used by others and make a positive difference.

From novice to software engineer, that’s the character arc I’ve created for myself in the Story of Ting. I’ve geared my education to a career in technology and now that I’m a graduate, I want to utilise everything I’ve learnt over the years, learn even more and improve my current skill set. This is because I want to develop great software for real clients, real people.
